12:Tc32,<h1>Learning Singapore‚Äôs Birds by Ear: A Look at the SingBirds Call Quiz</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>SingBirds Call Quiz is an interactive web application that challenges players to identify bird species by their songs. The game is centered around birds found in <strong>Singapore</strong>, making it both a fun and educational experience.</p>
<p>Players view a <strong>spectrogram</strong> of a bird call and listen to the audio recording before choosing the correct bird species from multiple-choice options. After submitting an answer, they can view detailed information and a photo of the bird.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Features</h2>
<ul>
<li>üéß <strong>Bird Call Audio</strong>: Listen to real bird recordings.</li>
<li>üìà <strong>Spectrogram Display</strong>: Visualize bird calls using spectrograms.</li>
<li>‚ùì <strong>Multiple-Choice Questions</strong>: Pick the correct species from a list of options.</li>
<li>üìù <strong>Detailed Feedback</strong>:
<ul>
<li>See which birds you identified correctly or incorrectly.</li>
<li>Access bird information via Wikipedia API after answering.</li>
<li>View bird images dynamically pulled from Wikipedia.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>üåé <strong>Hotspot and Country Selection</strong>: Select specific countries (currently only Singapore) and birding hotspots.</li>
<li>üéöÔ∏è <strong>Adjustable Quiz Settings</strong>: Set the number of questions.</li>
<li>üìä <strong>Quiz Result Summary</strong>: Review your score, accuracy, and species performance.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>Tech Stack</h2>
<h3>Frontend</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://react.dev/">React</a></li>
<li><a href="https://tailwindcss.com/">Tailwind CSS</a> for styling</li>
</ul>
<h3>Backend</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.djangoproject.com/">Django</a> with Django REST Framework</li>
<li>Custom APIs serving species lists, hotspot data, and bird observation records</li>
</ul>
<h3>Audio Processing</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://librosa.org/">Librosa</a> for audio analysis</li>
<li><a href="https://matplotlib.org/">Matplotlib</a> for spectrogram generation</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>Data Sources</h2>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>eBird API</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Retrieves Singapore hotspots</li>
<li>Gets bird species observed at hotspots over the past 30 days (snapshot taken in October)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Xeno-Canto API</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Provides bird song recordings</li>
<li>Filters recordings with <strong>Quality A</strong> and durations between <strong>0‚Äì30 seconds</strong></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Wikipedia API</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fetches species descriptions and images dynamically</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>App Flow</h2>
<ol>
<li>Player selects a country (currently only Singapore) and a hotspot.</li>
<li>App retrieves a list of birds based on recent observations (snapshot taken in Oct 2024).</li>
<li>Spectrogram and audio are displayed.</li>
<li>Player submits an answer.</li>
<li>App shows feedback, Wikipedia information, and bird photo.</li>
<li>After the quiz, a final score and breakdown are shown.</li>
</ol>
